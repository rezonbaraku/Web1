History about the Colosseum

Colosseum construction

70-72 CE

The Colosseum construction started around 72 CE during the reign of Emperor Vespasian of the Flavian dynasty. Vespasian considered it a gift to the Roman people. Vespasian wanted to build the Colosseum as a way to revitalize Rome after a very dark period.
Vespasian decided to replace the emperor's private lake for a public amphitheater that could accommodate tens of thousands of Romans. In the Colosseum, the public could enjoy gladiatorial battles, other forms of entertainment and also show the world the power and the might of Rome.

The Grand opening

80 CE

The Colosseum finished constructions in a relatively short amount of time considering the size of the Colosseum and also the type of equipment used. In 80 CE the Colosseum was officially open to the public.
The person to open the Colosseum was Vespasian's son Titus, the original name was the Flavian amphitheater and to celebrate the opening Titus declared 100 days of games, which included gladiatorial battles and animal fights. Over 50,000 spectators came and watched the games.

The Colosseum Over the Centuries

200-1700

The usage of Colosseum changed overtime. For about four centuries the Colosseum saw active use however with the struggles of the Roman empire and the gradual change of public taste, around the 6th century the Colosseum saw the end of wild animals fights and the end of gladiatorial battles.
After that, the Colosseum was used as a church and place for public political debates till eventually around the 18th century it was completed abandoned. The Colosseum had suffered damage from earthquakes, lightnings and was used as quarry for different building projects

19th Century - Present day

1901-2023

In the 19th Century various popes, raised the issue that the Colosseum should be protected as a holy Christian site. Unfortunately, by the 20th Century a combination of carelessness, weather and natural disasters had destroyed almost two-thirds of the Colosseum.
Restoration work started in the 1990s and has continued as till this day. Today the Colosseum remains a popular destination for travelers from across the world.
