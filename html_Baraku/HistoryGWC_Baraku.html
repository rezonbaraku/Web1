History about the Great Wall of China

Early History

700 BCE

The construction of the Great Wall of China started very early ( 7th century BCE). The first part of the wall was built by the state of Chu as a lasting defensive system. This was known as the “Square Wall”. The barricade was located in kingdom’s capital province at the northern area.
Later on ( 6th to the 4th century BCE) other states started to follow in the footsteps of Chu’s. In the southern part of the Qi state an extensive barricade was slowly build. This barricade was build using existing river dikes, newly constructed bulwarks, and areas of impenetrable mountain terrain. This is the first time where the surrounding environment was used in building a defensive system.
Shi Huangdi ( 221 BCE ) was able to unify China. After this he appointed General Meng Tian to links the wall already build as a defense against Xiongnu. After, the shift of power plans of building it were abandoned and the wall was left in a bad condition.

The Han through Yuan dynasties

100 BCE - 13 Century

Han emperor Wudi (141–87 BCE), decided to continue with the construction of the wall and during his reign it was strengthened. A 20-year project on how to strengthen the wall started in 121 BCE The plan of the construction was to a beacon every 5 li( li is a Chinese unit of distance 1 li is approximately 0.5 km) a tower every 10 li a fort every 30 li and a castle to be build every 100 li.
During different reigns and periods, the Wall was constantly repaired, new fortifications were being built and enlarged as a way to defend from various attacks from different tribes. In 555 1.8 million workers were sent to extend and repair the Wall. The Great Wall did also serve as way to control and centralize trade and travel.
The Wall had been pretty successflul until the 13 Century where Genghis Khan's dynasty (Yuan) were able to break in, invade and control all of China.

The Ming dynasty

1368–1644

The early Ming rulers that came after the Yuan dynasty shared the same mindset as the Yuan dynasty. They showed very little interest in maintaining and strengthening the Great Wall until the late 15 centuries. In 1421 Beijing became the capital of China.
In 1474 the Ming dynasty started to focus less in expansion and attack and more in defense. So, during this time the Wall was constantly maintained, strengthened and enlarged. The main reason behind this was stop and prevent another Mongolian invasion. Most of the Great Wall that we see and stands today is the work done during the reign of the Hongzhi emperor ( 1487–1505)

Qing dynasty - Present day

1644-2023

After the Ming dynasty was replaced by the Qing dynasty there was a change in strategy. The Qing dynasty ( 1644-1912 ) were concentrated more in building peaceful relationship with the Mongolians and other tribes rather than building barriers and fortifications. This was the reason the Wall was not constantly maintained, strengthened and enlarged and why it gradually started to ruin.
Today The Great Wall of China is preserved and constantly maintained. The purpose it serves today is as a large historical tourist attraction.
