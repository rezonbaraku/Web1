Information about The Colosseum

General informatiom about The Colosseum

The purpose of The Colosseum was to revitalize Rome after a dark period, to build a massive structure to show the rest of the world the might, strength, power and wealth of Rome. The construction of The Colosseum started around the year 70 CE. Throughout various periods The Colosseum was used for a variety of things. The Colosseum was used to hold political speeches, animal hunts, public executions, dramas and most famously gladiator battles. When The Colosseum first opened the emperor Titus decided to celebrate by holding 100 days of gladiator games.
It is considered the largest amphitheater ever built. It is over 48 meters tall and could hold more than 50,000 spectators.
In 1980 The Colosseum became a UNESCO World Heritage Site and in 2007 it was officially declared as one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.

Intriguing things to try when visiting The Colosseum

Underground Tour

If you want to experience The Colosseum in a very rare and unique way then visiting the underground tunnels is a must. In this tour, you can see the trap doors, walk in the arena floor, view the gladiators gate, view the wild animal chambers and the elevator systems.
The demand is very high so the tickets must be booked months in advance if you want a close up experience of The Colosseum.

Learn to Be a Gladiator

This is for people interested in the gladiator games and want to experience what being a gladiator is like. During a hands-on lesson, you learn how to use and fight using authentic weapons that the gladiator used while also wearing traditonal gladiator helmets and outfits.
During this 2-hour training you will have a lot of fun, experience something unique while also learning about ancient Roman history.

Learn more

Night Tour

If you want to experience The Colosseum when it is less crowded and more secluded then entering the monument at night is for you. During the night tour you can walk in The Colosseum eerily-lit and looming corridors while listening to stories of Rome's legendary gladiators.
This tour is something you will remember for the rest of your life and if you have the possibility to do it don't miss it.
